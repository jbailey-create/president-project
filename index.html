📋 Evaluation Rubric

Content & Historical Thinking (20 pts)

  • 20: Clearly explains how rights were helped or harmed with strong insight and specific details.
  • 15: Explains impact on rights with some detail and understanding.
  • 10: Basic explanation, lacks detail or clarity.
  • 5 or below: Limited or unclear understanding.

Organization (20 pts)

  • 20: Clear intro, thesis with 3 prongs, organized body, strong conclusion.
  • 15: Mostly organized, minor issues.
  • 10: Some organization, hard to follow.
  • 5 or below: Disorganized.

Evidence (20 pts)

  • 20: Strong, accurate evidence (laws, actions, events) clearly supports argument.
  • 15: Some good evidence, generally supports ideas.
  • 10: Limited or unclear evidence.
  • 5 or below: Little to no evidence.

Visuals (20 pts)

  • 20: Slides are clear, engaging, and support ideas (not text-heavy).
  • 15: Mostly clear and organized slides.
  • 10: Basic slides, too much text.
  • 5 or below: Slides unclear or distracting.

Presentation Skills (40 pts)

  • 40: Speaks clearly, maintains strong eye contact, presents for full time (around 5 minutes), and does NOT read from notecards or slides.
  • 30: Mostly clear speaking, some eye contact, minor reliance on notes, close to time requirement.
  • 20: Frequent reading from notes/slides, limited eye contact, presentation is too short or rushed.
  • 10 or below: Mostly read directly from notes/slides, little eye contact, unclear or unprepared.

Total: /120

Student Success Checklist ✅

  • I clearly answered the essential question
  • My thesis states if my president helped, harmed, or both
  • My thesis includes 3 clear arguments (prongs)
  • I focused on a specific group (or groups) of people
  • I included strong evidence (laws, actions, events)
  • I explained HOW rights were affected
  • I considered different perspectives (not just one side)
  • My slides are clear and not full of text
  • I used my own words (no copying!)
  • I practiced speaking without reading
